Autonomous Car Project Summary

Identify the Project:

Autonomous Car Navigation System utilizing an Arduino UNO microcontroller.

Describe the Project:

Before coming to the US, during my time at St. Xavier's College in Kathmandu, I wanted to build something tangible that combined my growing interests in hardware and coding. The goal of this project was to build a miniature self-driving car that could dynamically navigate a room without bumping into obstacles. We used ultrasonic sensors as the vehicle's "eyes" to detect distances, and an Arduino UNO as the central brain to process that data and steer the motors in real-time.

Identify Skills Acquired:

This was one of my first major collaborative builds. As the Robotics Team Lead Captain for a group of six, I learned how to manage a project timeline, delegate tasks, and troubleshoot as a team. Technically, I dove deep into C++ programming. I took open-source libraries from GitHub and heavily modified them to fit our specific hardware setup, which taught me how to read, adapt, and optimize existing codebases to solve my own engineering problems.

Importance/Application:

While this was a small-scale build, the fundamental logic we used—pinging sensors to detect surroundings and adjusting trajectory autonomously—is the exact same foundational concept behind modern self-driving cars and warehouse robotics. Building this gave me a very real, hands-on appreciation for the complex sensor fusion algorithms being developed to make our roads safer and logistics more efficient today.
